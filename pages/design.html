CONCLUSION

From the two tables, we can conclude that:

  1. A similarity across the resource is that each resource used conceptual learning to teach
    the content of Newton’s First Law of Motion; however, the cognitive process and
    knowledge dimension that have been involved are quite different between the textbook
    one and the other two (Table 2). The field trip video goes as far as applying & analyzing
    the knowledge although the target learners are just elementary students, whereas the
    textbook resource, aiming for high schoolers, stops at only understanding the knowledge
    and transitions the pressure of involving higher cognitive processes to the instructors. We
    believe that every learner of different ages can achieve higher cognitive processes when
    their learning experience is exquisitely designed.
  2. All the three materials were based on the same learning content : Newton’s first law of
    motion (Table 1). But after analyzing, the field-trip video and the multimedia one appear
    to be generally more engaging, and therefore have a better chance to achieve the
    instructional goals and generate better learning outcomes by incorporating visuals.
    However, when using multimedia, it is also important to be cautious that incorporating
    visuals can create extraneous cognitive load and affect learning experience if without
    clear objectives and delicate designs.
