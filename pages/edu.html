    MY ROLE

    While we worked in a group of three on the entire project, we used the strategy “Divide &
    Conquer” by assigning sections on an individual basis and then reviewing together to create an
    outstanding product.
    I worked mostly on the big summary of the game including educational objectives, creating
    personas, conducting market analysis, proposing game rules and describing the logic model of
    the game based on cognitive, affective, social & narrative design principles.

    INTRODUCTION

    For Context the state government gave our company Gaming Inc. the contract of designing a
    game on waste management and of launching a long-term competition (based on the game)
    with a prize money - an educational scholarship of $1000. The game is targeted to promote an
    attitudinal shift towards green habits amongst the middle school students in the long term.
      We are currently living in a world where environmental sustainability is at the forefront of
    the future. An environmental crisis looms in our lives and every person can do their part to
    make sure that our environment is clean, even in the smallest daily activities. This game wants
    to teach students what they can do in their lives, now and in the future, to help the world. The
    main idea of this game is to teach about the key parts of reuse, reduce, and recycle in real life
    scenarios and bring a behavioral & attitudinal change in their actions. Players will go through
    the game and learn what products to recycle, what products can be reused, and what products
    must be thrown away. The players already know the concept of recycling but they do not know
    how to apply it in the real world. So, the purpose of our game is to apply the knowledge they
    already possess and take actions.
